I am a Ph.D candidate in Programming Languages and Software Engineering at Purdue CS. My advisor is Prof. Suresh Jagannathan. The focus of my current research is on building effective programming abstractions and reasoning techniques for systems with weak consistency guarantees, such as NoSQL stores (check out Quelea and Q9) and non-ACID relational databases. I have previously worked on dependent type systems for functional languages (see Catalyst) and region type systems for stream processing languages. I frequently blog my research notes here.

Previously, I spent some time building publishing tools at Yahoo! India. I earned M.S in CS from Purdue in 2016, and B.E in CS from BITS, Pilani, India in 2009.

I am a 2018 Google PhD Fellow in Programming Technology and Software Engineering

I am currently on the academic/industry-research job market.
